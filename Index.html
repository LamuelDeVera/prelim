Intro

The Philippine government is working to restore normalcy in the Philippines after decades of seeking and serving the country. It's been two years since the Philippines returned to normalcy, but since a virus known as Covid-19 spread throughout the world, and it's not the first time we've had the virus because the history of human coronaviruses began in 1965, when Tyrrell and Bynoe discovered that they could pass a virus known as B814.

The government has begun to loosen limitations. As an outcome, companies may run at full capacity, and individuals of all ages and comorbidities can travel freely. According to pcoo.gov.ph, the Philippines is preparing to return to "normal" following a large immunization—vaccine campaign. The country, which is still at low danger from the coronavirus, had a daily average of 389 cases from March 21 to 27, down 24% from the previous week. The Philippines will remain on alert level 1 for the time being, with some limitations in place in specific areas..

Progress

The Philippines' economy would likely decrease by 8.1 percent in 2020 as a result of various shocks, including the COVID-19 health crisis, economic operations across the nation being halted by quarantine measures, damaging typhoons in November, and the global recession. The Department of Health (DOH) has designated 2021 as the year of recovery from the current epidemic, including lessons learned from 2020. It was also the year that Filipinos saw hope and an end to the epidemic with the delivery of life-saving doses in February, thanks to a Chinese government donation of Sinovac Covid-19 vaccine.Infections with COVID-19 are declining in the Philippines, with an average of 324 new infections recorded per day. That's a tenth of what it was on January 18, when the greatest day average was registered. Since the outbreak began, the country has seen 3,680,514 illnesses and 59,591 coronavirus-related fatalities.

Until the end of March, Metro Manila and 47 other localities are on Alert Level 1. Establishments and public transit are allowed to operate normally under Alert Level 1, which the administration refers to as the "new normal." There are no limits on the migration of persons of various ages, albeit this is subject to particular norms that vary each local government unit. The Philippine government is doing everything possible to return the Philippines to a virus-free state.
